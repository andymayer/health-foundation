Having an interpreter two days a week in a population with a high number of Bengali speakers

Purpose: To share emerging practice and learn lessons

{% include contributed-by.html %}

What was the problem

Our practice has a majority Bengali-speaking population, regularly needing interpreters. Oneâ€™s booked externally would often not show, and the usefulness varied. Language line consultations have many challenges.

How did we approach it

We all felt we would benefit from a regular, reliable interpreter for both the clinical staff and the patients.

What did we do

We hired an experienced Bengali interpreter to be in the practice as standard two days a week.

Did it work?

It has made a fantastic difference to both patients and staff. Obviously in an emergency e can use language line, but for routine appointments we are able to have clear discussions. In addition, because the same interpreter is now present, the patients get to know her and vis a versa, resulting in a rapport building up between all the team.

Recommendation

This has significantly decreased our workload from misunderstanding, missed appointments, and language line, and improved patient care.
